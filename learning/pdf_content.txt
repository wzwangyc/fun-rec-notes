A B S T R A C T
Stock price sequence prediction is highly challenging due to the high noise, non-stationarity, and
complex high-order interdependencies in financial time series. The existing deep learning models en-
counter difficulties in clearly capturing the group-level synergy effects beyond pairwise relationships,
and also lack adaptability to different market environments as well as the interpretability required
for practical decision-making. This paper proposes Meta-HCapsNet, an attention-enhanced capsule
network incorporating hypergraph structure for stock price forecasting. The model introduces three
key innovations: a lightweight Meta-Adapter that dynamically generates hypergraph construction
parameters and capsule routing priors based on input statistical features to enable rapid meta-learning
adaptation; a novel Hypergraph Capsule Routing layer that embeds high-order group correlations into
the iterative dynamic routing process of capsules; and an adaptive attention pooling mechanism that
provides intuitive interpretability through activation patterns. Extensive experiments on three real-
world Chinese stock datasets from BYD, CATL, and CSSC demonstrate that Meta-HCapsNet consis-
tently outperforms seven state-of-the-art baseline models, including iTransformer and MEAformer,
achieving up to 54% reduction in MAE as well as significant reductions in RMSE and MAPE across
short- and long-term horizons. Ablation studies confirm the effectiveness of each component, few-
shot adaptation experiments verify superior generalization with limited data, and interpretability
analysis shows that capsule activation intensities strongly correspond to meaningful market phases
such as rising and retracement, demonstrating the model’s accuracy, adaptability, and transparency in
modeling complex financial dynamics.
1. Introduction
Financial time series prediction has always been a re-
search hotspot in the interdisciplinary field of artificial in-
telligence and financial engineering due to its core role in
quantitative investment, risk hedging, and market super-
vision. However, the financial market, as a complex dy-
namical system, exhibits extremely high levels of noise,
non-stationarity, and multi-factor coupling, making it ex-
tremely challenging to accurately capture the patterns of
price fluctuations[1, 2]. Traditional statistical and econo-
metric models and early machine learning methods often
encounter limitations in extracting deep market dynamics
features due to the bottleneck of manual feature engineering
when dealing with massive high-dimensional and nonlinear
financial data[3].
In recent years, the rapid development of deep learning
technology has completely changed this situation. Long
Short-Term Memory networks (LSTM) and their variants
once dominated sequential modeling, but their serial com-
puting characteristics limited their ability to capture long-
term dependencies[4]. Subsequently, the Transformer archi-
tecture achieved breakthroughs in long sequence prediction
tasks through the self-attention mechanism. For instance,

PatchTST[5] proposed patching time series to retain lo-

Additionally, DeepONet[7]and various hybrid models[8]
have also been proposed to further enhance the model’s

Although Transformer-based models excel in capturing
long-range temporal dependencies, they often overlook the
complex spatial topological structure among financial enti-
ties. Stocks in the financial market do not fluctuate indepen-
dently but form a closely interconnected network through
supply chains, equity relationships, or macro policies. To
address this issue, graph neural networks (GNNs) have
been widely adopted, such as STGAT[9] and CIRGNN[10],
which explicitly model the pairwise relationships between
stocks using graph attention mechanisms. However, the
interconnections in the real market often exhibit "group
synergy" effects, such as collective movements within the
same concept sector. This higher-order dependency beyond
pairwise relationships cannot be effectively described by
simple graph structures. Therefore, the hypergraph theory
has gained attention. Recent studies, such as FactorGCL[11],
indicate that hypergraph structures can more accurately
capture the complex nonlinear group relationships among
multiple nodes.

accuracy, often encounter two key drawbacks: one is their

lack of adaptability, making it difficult to cope with the
rapid changes in market styles. The second issue is the
lack of interpretability. The "black box" nature of deep
neural networks severely hinders their credibility in financial
decision-making. To address the aforementioned challenges,
this paper presents a meta-learning capsule network with
a hypergraph structure(Meta-HCapsNet) for stock sequence

Firstly, a lightweight Meta-Adapter is designed based on the

encodes the high-order group relationships between time
points, compensating for the shortcomings of traditional
Transformers and GNNs in modeling high-order correla-
tions; Finally, an adaptive attention pooling mechanism is
implemented for decoding from high-level capsules to global
predictions, and the activation patterns of capsules provide
intuitive explanations of market states.
2. Literature review
Stock market prediction, as an interdisciplinary field
combining financial engineering and computational intelli-
gence, is characterized by its complexity due to the high
noise in market microstructure, the nonlinear transmission
mechanism of macroeconomic factors, and the group irra-
tionality of investor behavior[12, 13]. In recent years, with
the continuous evolution of deep learning architectures, the
mature application of graph neural network theory, and
the breakthrough development of generative artificial in-
telligence technology, stock prediction research has shifted
from traditional single-variable time series modeling to the
construction of a systematic framework for multimodal data
fusion, dynamic relationship graph modeling, and inter-
pretable intelligent decision-making.
2.1. Traditional machine learning methods
The traditional machine learning research for stock pre-
diction mainly relied on shallow models such as Support
Vector Machines (SVM)[14] and Random Forests (RF)[15,
16], and focused on feature optimization and integration
strategies. For instance, Nti et al.[17] evaluated the compre-
hensive effectiveness of ensemble learning in stock predic-
tion in the Journal of Big Data and found that the Stack-
ing method was significantly superior to a single model.
Alotaibi[18] proposed a hybrid feature selection method
based on red deer-gray wolf optimization, achieving a 9.2%
accuracy improvement in the Saudi stock market.
Although ensemble learning significantly enhances the
robustness of predictions through model combination, its
effectiveness is highly dependent on the quality of input
features. Therefore, feature engineering, as another core
pillar determining the upper limit of model performance,
has achieved breakthrough progress in recent years with
the empowerment of deep learning[19]. Long et al.[20]
proposed a deep learning feature engineering framework
that broke through the limitations of traditional technical
indicators’ linear combination. It automatically extracted
high-order abstract features of the price sequence through
the Restricted Boltzmann Machine (RBM). Paiva et al.[21]
in Expert Systems with Applications restructured the feature
engineering process from the perspective of portfolio opti-
mization, combining the Markowitz mean-variance frame-
work with machine learning prediction. Through the output
yield distribution of the model, the optimal weight vector
was directly generated. In an empirical study at the São Paulo
Stock Exchange in Brazil, this integrated method increased
the annualized return of the strategy.
2.2. Deep learning methods
While feature engineering lays a solid foundation of
high-quality data for the model, the continuous innovation of
the deep learning architecture itself has become another core
engine driving breakthroughs in prediction performance.
In recent years, it has achieved leapfrog development in
three dimensions: long short-term memory network(LSTM)
optimization, convolutional neural network (CNN) spatio-
temporal modeling, and Transformer paradigm innovation.
The LSTM has further consolidated its dominant posi-
tion in time series modeling due to its gating mechanism.
Saurabh[22] was the first to apply LSTM to predict the
daily stock prices of a specific company. Compared with
traditional machine learning and artificial neural network
models, it was verified that LSTM can accept and con-
sider the sequence of input data, which is conducive to the
persistence of the data. Md et al.[23] innovatively adopted
a three-layer stacked architecture of sequential LSTM and
introduced the sine cosine algorithm (SCA) to optimize the
hyperparameters of the initial learning rate and batch size. In
the daily price predictions of three technology stocks (Apple,
Google, and Microsoft), it achieved a reduction of over 60%
compared to traditional LSTM.
After the convolutional neural network (CNN) was
transferred from computer vision to financial time series
analysis, its local receptive field and parameter sharing
characteristics have demonstrated unique advantages. The
BiCuDNNLSTM-1dCNN hybrid model proposed by Kan-
wal et al.[24] represents the current technological fron-
tier. This architecture uses bidirectional CUDA-accelerated
LSTM to extract long-term dependencies, followed by 1D-
CNN to capture short-term price patterns. On real datasets,
it outperforms a single LSTM model by 9% and reduces
training time by 40%. Prasad et al.[25] explored the di-
mension expansion of CNN and designed 2D-CNN to
process price-volume heat maps, and 3D-CNN to construct a
three-dimensional tensor of time-price-technical indicators.
Through the three-dimensional representation of 82 techni-
cal features, the prediction accuracy was improved by 3-11%
on Nasdaq 100 component stocks.

The self-attention mechanism of the Transformer model
has completely transformed the traditional time-series mod-
eling approach by demonstrating its superiority in handling
long-range dependencies. Sun et al.[26] developed a dual-
channel CNN-LSTM-Attention fusion model that employs
a multi-head attention mechanism to dynamically allocate
weights for different time steps. In the CSI300 index pre-
diction, this model outperformed the traditional model by

MEAformer proposed by Huang et al.[27] discard the tradi-

is decreased by 50%.
2.3. Graph neural network methods
The inter-industry correlations, supply chain relation-
ships, and investor co-investment patterns among stocks
form a complex dynamic graph structure. Kim and Park
[28] proposed STAD-GCN, which captures the evolution
of the graph structure through a time attention mecha-
nism. Each trading day builds a stock correlation graph,
spatial graph convolution aggregates neighbor informa-
tion, and time convolution captures sequence dynamics,
achieving higher accuracy compared to static graph mod-
els. Yang et al. [29] constructed the MDF-DMC model,
which introduces multi-view features and updates dynamic
market correlations through gated recurrent units (GRUs).
The prediction accuracy on cross-industry stock pools in-
creased by 6.8%, demonstrating the effectiveness of het-
erogeneous information fusion. Xiang et al.[30] proposed
the Time-Heterogeneous Heterogeneous Graph Neural Net-
work (THGNN) treating stocks, industries, regions, and
concepts as heterogeneous nodes. It captures high-order
correlations of "stock - industry - stock" through meta-
paths. In the prediction of the entire A-share market, this
model reduces the prediction error for small and medium-
sized stocks, proving its advantage in data-sparse scenar-
ios. Huynh et al.[31] proposed the Multi-order Dynamics
Integration model, which integrates first-order and second-
order dynamics. It learns individual stock-specific patterns
through an internal dynamic encoder (IDE) and provides
new ideas for high-frequency trading.
3. Methodology
The overall architecture of Meta-HCapsNet is a hier-
archical information processing flow, as shown in Figure
1. The model starts with raw multi-dimensional financial
time series data, first extracting local features through a
one-dimensional convolutional layer, and then converting
them into primary capsule representations containing pose
information. Next, a lightweight meta-adapter analyzes the
statistical features of the primary capsule sequence, dy-
namically generating hypergraph construction parameters
and capsule routing prior parameters. These parameters are
then sent to the hypergraph capsule routing layer-the core
innovative module of this model, which deeply integrates
the hypergraph structure into the dynamic routing process
of capsules, generating high-level capsules capable of repre-
senting complex high-order patterns through iterative opti-
mization. Finally, the high-level capsules are aggregated into
a global representation through an adaptive attention pooling
mechanism and multi-step predictions are made through
a multi-layer perceptron. The entire process achieves end-
to-end learning from raw data to prediction results, while
maintaining the structured and interpretable nature of the
internal computational process.

In order to effectively train the meta-adaptor and en-

adaptor generates parameters Θ𝑖. The model uses Θ𝑖to

4.2. Robustness Analysis across Extended
Forecasting Horizons
To rigorously evaluate the long-term predictive stability
and performance attenuation of Meta-HCapsNet, we ex-
panded the forecasting horizon 𝑃from the initial 5 days to a
range of {5, 10, 20, 30, 40} trading days. The historical look-
back window 𝑇was maintained at 30 days to examine how
the model handles increasing uncertainty with limited histor-
ical input. We compared Meta-HCapsNet against two state-
of-the-art baselines, iTransformer and MEAformer, across
three datasets.
Figure 4 shows the changes in MAE for these three
models as the prediction period lengthens. Firstly, it can
be observed that the upward trend of Meta-HCapsNet is
the most gradual. Taking BYD as an example, the MAE
of iTransformer increased by approximately 123.2% from
𝑃= 5 to 𝑃= 40, while Meta-HCapsNet only increased
by 107.5%. This directly proves that the high-order group
correlation captured by the hypergraph capsule routing is
more capable of resisting the non-stationarity and noise
interference of financial data compared to the traditional
point-to-point Attention.
Secondly, it can be observed that on the highly fluc-
tuating CATL dataset, the differences among the various
models become increasingly obvious as 𝑃increases. The
dynamically generated routing prior Θ𝑟by Meta-Adapter
plays a crucial role here. It can adjust the parameters in
real time based on the statistical characteristics of the input
sequence, enabling the model to still accurately adapt to
the drift of market styles in long-term predictions. On the
CSSC dataset, when 𝑃= 40, Meta-HCapsNet still keeps
the MAE at a low level, while the comparison models have
all exceeded 6.0. This stable performance indicates that
the capsule network’s encoding ability for entity posture
information is more resistant to attenuation compared to
simple scalar feature fusion in multi-step predictions.

is critical for handling market non-stationarity and stock
heterogeneity, as fixed parameters fail to adapt effectively
to different volatility patterns. Replacing hypergraph with
pairwise graph leads to moderate-to-large degradation, with
average MAE increase of 6-10%, and the effect is particu-

advantage of hypergraphs over fully connected graphs in
capturing multi-node higher-order relationships such as in-

sule routing produces one of the largest drops, with MAE
increase of 38.5% on CSSC, highlighting the irreplaceable
role of dynamic routing iterations in encoding high-level
semantic capsules with entity pose and existence probability,
while standard multilayer perceptron aggregation loses the
interpretable feature composition process. Removing atten-
tion pooling results in stable but noticeable degradation,
with average MAE increase of 4-6%, as adaptive atten-
tion effectively highlights key group capsules under cur-
rent market conditions, whereas average pooling produces
overly smoothed global representations and reduces predic-
tion precision. Removing high-order aggregation shows sig-

increase of 11.5% on CATL, confirming that integrating
multi-node information within hyperedges is essential for
modeling collective movement patterns, and relying solely
on individual node features severely weakens group synergy
capture. Removing reconstruction loss produces the smallest
yet consistent degradation, with average MAE increase of 3-
5% across all datasets, as the auxiliary constraint substan-
tially improves routing stability and convergence quality,
preventing local oscillations that arise from unconstrained
iteration.

4.5. Interpretability Analysis
Although quantitative metrics demonstrate the superior
predictive performance of Meta-HCapsNet, its capsule-
based architecture and hypergraph integration also provide
rich interpretability, addressing a critical limitation of exist-
ing black-box deep learning models in financial applications.
In this section, we qualitatively analyze the internal repre-
sentations of the model to illustrate how high-level capsules
and hypergraph structures offer intuitive explanations of
market states and decision-making processes.
The magnitude of each high-level capsule vector explic-
itly represents the existence probability of a specific high-
order market pattern. We visualize the activation strengths
of all high-level capsules over a representative 30-day input
window on the BYD dataset, during which the stock expe-
rienced a clear upward trend followed by a short correction
As shown in Figure 6, capsules 2, 5, and 8 exhibit
consistently high activation during the rising phase, suggest-
ing they encode bullish patterns such as sustained buying
pressure or positive sector momentum. In contrast, capsule
4 activates strongly during the correction phase, potentially
corresponding to risk-averse or profit-taking behaviors. This
pattern is consistent across multiple windows: capsules with
high activation during upward movements tend to have pose
vectors that align with increasing trend intensity statistics
extracted by the Meta-Adapter.

5. Conclusion
In this study, we proposed Meta-HCapsNet, a novel
attention-enhanced capsule network with hypergraph struc-
ture to tackle the challenges of stock price sequence fore-
casting, including high noise, non-stationarity, and complex
high-order interdependencies in financial time series. The
model integrates three key innovations: a lightweight Meta-
Adapter for rapid meta-learning adaptation, a Hypergraph
: Preprint submitted to Elsevier
Page 12 of 14
Capsule Routing layer for modeling group-level correla-
tions, and an adaptive attention pooling mechanism for
improved interpretability. Experiments on three real-world
Chinese stock datasets (BYD, CATL, and CSSC) show that
Meta-HCapsNet consistently outperforms seven state-of-
the-art baselines, including iTransformer and MEAformer,
achieving up to 54% reduction in MAE and substantial
improvements in RMSE and MAPE across various horizons.
Ablation studies confirm the effectiveness of each compo-
nent, few-shot experiments demonstrate strong generaliza-
tion with limited data, and interpretability analysis reveals
capsule activations aligning with meaningful market phases
such as rising and retracement.
These results highlight Meta-HCapsNet’s superior ac-
curacy, adaptability, and transparency in modeling complex
financial dynamics. Future work will explore extensions to
multivariate forecasting, integration of multimodal data such
as news sentiment, and applications to other non-stationary
domains.
